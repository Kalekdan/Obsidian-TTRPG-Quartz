One-shot wild west campaign run by [[Tom]]

>The Wild West - 1887 was a vast and rugged region stretching from the Great Plains to the Pacific Ocean, encompassing areas that would later become states such as Texas, California, and Montana. The land was characterised by sweeping vistas, towering mountains, and endless prairies, but it was also marked by harsh climate conditions and unpredictable weather patterns that could spell disaster for those unprepared.  
>
>Moreover, the region was home to various Native American tribes, who often clashed with settlers over land and resources, leading to fierce battles and deep-seated animosity. Settlers, in turn, brought their own cultural and societal norms to the region, which clashed with those of the indigenous peoples. This cultural divide would come to define the Wild West for years to come, and its legacy is still felt in the region today.
>
  >
>The discovery of gold in California in 1848 triggered a massive influx of migrants to the region, including prospectors, entrepreneurs, and fortune-seekers. As a result, boomtowns sprang up almost overnight, with saloons, brothels, and gambling dens catering to the miners and other fortune-seekers. This influx of people also led to increased lawlessness, as outlaws and bandits took advantage of the lack of government control to commit crimes such as train robberies and cattle rustling.
>
>The Country is boiling towards a civil war that will set the course of the nation for the next thousand years.
>
>Overall, the Wild West in 1850 was a land of contradictions, with opportunities for wealth and adventure, but also dangers lurking around every corner. It was a place where people had to rely on their wits, their skills, and their courage to survive, and where the line between hero and villain was often blurred.
