Vowel harmony refers to the fact that in many languages, the vowel sounds align throughout the word. e.g. if a root contains multiple sounds at the front of the mouth, the rest of the word may contain the same (or vice versa). See an example below for Hungarian of Backness/Palatal Harmony:

| Root    | Dative          | Gloss  |
| ------- | --------------- | ------ |
| _város_ | _város**-nak**_ | 'city' |
| _öröm_  | _öröm**-nek**_  | 'joy'  |
[[Ilkura]] makes use of Palatal Harmony based on the last vowel sound in the root of the noun.

When the last vowel sound is x, x, or x, the vowel sound in the suffix is y. Otherwise the vowel sound in the suffix is z.
