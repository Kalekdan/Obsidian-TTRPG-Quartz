Noun Classes refers to the grouping of nouns, such as masculine and feminine in most indo-european languages (though some argue masculine and feminine are their own thing as 'gramatical gender'). For the case of [[Ilkura]], there are only a few noun classes, based on the nouns ability/capacity to create sound. These are shown by the suffix on the noun as given below


| Class Number | Prefix        | Associated Meaning                                                                                 | Example            |
| ------------ | ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| I            | -kha          | Resonants (creatures with the ability to speak or create sound)                                    | Person, Dog,       |
| II           | -sakh         | Silents (living creatures without the ability to speak or create sound)                            | Worm, Tree         |
| III          | -swhu (s-who) | Whisperbound (resonants who choose not to/can't make sound - usually, members of the whisperbound) | Whisperbound, Mute |
| IV           | -ul           | Abstract objects, concepts or actions (e.g. Dream, Dance)                                          | Dream              |
