###  Intro 
The party are gathered in a tavern, having some drinks when they hear a commotion outside, including a sheep "baa'ing". A sheep wonders into the tavern with a scroll in it's mouth and surveys the patrons of the inn. He approaches the party, and the scroll turns out to be a scroll of 'speak with animals'. The scroll is activated, and the party can communicate with the sheep.

It turns out, the sheep was once a wizard, who was betrayed by his apprentice and turned into a sheep. There is more commotion outside, and in walks and tall half-orc, with a wolf with an iron collar at his side. 3 more people follow him in, each with wolves, including a huge hooded figure, 10-15ft tall.

The half-orc, Guz, approaches the group and tells them that the sheep belongs to Master Noke, and he wishes to take it back. As the group can speak to the sheep, they are hesitant to return him, as they understand him to be a person. Eventually Guz loses his patience, and attempts to take the sheep by force.

A fight ensues, during which the large hooded figure is revealed to be a bear, walking on it's hind legs. After the party manage to defeat the intruders, someone finds a note on Guz's body. It is a letter from a noble, requesting transmutation services, and a response from Ahmed Noke:
>'I appreciate your custom, but if you want my services you'll have to come to me'

The sheep tells the party that they need to find his sister Lillieth. He says they can't let Noke continue in his lab. They need to head south to his wizard tower. Noke was his apprentice and turned him into a sheep by mistake.

###  Princess Pumperninkle 
The party approach a bridge, and hear voices on the other side of it. Finethir (the sheep) suddenly stops, and stares at a rabbit; 'Is that my sister?'. With the ability to talk to animals, the party are aware that it is just a normal rabbit, so shoot it, much to Finethir's shock.

They continue over the bridge with the dead rabbit in hand, and see a whole field of rabbits, with some tents at the far side. There is also a girl playing with a rabbit on a string, calling her 'Princess Pumperninkle'. They approach her and ask for the rabbit, but she won't give it up. She's pulling on a string tight around the rabbit's neck. They manage to snatch the rabbit away, and attempt to replace it with the dead rabbit they have.

The people in the tents hear this commotion and come out to see the party pestering the child. A fight ensues, in which the party kill all the adults, and steal the horses. Before leaving, a couple of party members look through the tents to see if there is anything of interest, and find a baby crying in a crib. Only the child and the baby are left when the party head off with 'Princess Pumperninkle' the rabbit, and Finethir the sheep.
