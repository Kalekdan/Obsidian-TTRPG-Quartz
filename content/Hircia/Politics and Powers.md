[[Hircia]] is politically fragmented. The world has a handful of large powers, a number of smaller states, and a few regions that survive by balancing stronger neighbours against each other.
## Major powers

### The Kingdom of Errun
[[The King of Errun|Errun]] is one of the central continental powers in the era before the Convergence. It becomes a primary force after the Great War and is part of the broader political balance that shapes the known world.
### The Duchy of Bunelia
[[The Duchy of Bunelia|Bunelia]] rises as a major power through alliance and later becomes the dominant force after the Convergence. In the post-Convergence period, Bunelia is no longer just a duchy and instead becomes the core of a wider kingdom or empire depending on the era being described.
### The Empire of Kelpis
[[The Empire of Kelpis|Kelpis]] is a major rival power, especially in the south-west, where it contests territory, trade, and influence with Bunelia. (see [[Conflict between Bunelia and Kelpis]])
### The Principality of Sal
[[The Principality of Sal|Sal]] is a smaller but important power on the northern continent. It acts as a regional state rather than a world-spanning empire, which makes it useful as a political pressure point in local stories.
### The Free City of Nakur
[[The Free City of Nakur|Nakur]] stays independent by being politically useful, hard to absorb, and not worth the cost of direct conquest unless a stronger power is willing to spend the effort.

## Major tensions
### [[Conflict between Bunelia and Kelpis|Bunelia versus Kelpis]]
**(Pre-convergence)** This is one of the clearest long-term political conflicts in [[Hircia]]. The two powers compete over the gem-rich south-western jungles and over influence in adjacent regions.
### Bunelia versus the remnants of older orders
After the Convergence, Bunelia’s rise is not just territorial. It is also ideological. The empire’s control is enforced through fear, military force, and religious influence, making it the main hegemon of the post-Convergence world.
### Trade and smuggling
The world’s ports and routes matter as much as its armies. [[Roseport]],  [[the Thorny Coves]], and other trade nodes show that politics in [[Hircia]] is also about who controls commerce, not just who wears a crown.

## How political power works
* Military strength matters, but magical or monstrous power can rewrite the balance quickly.
* Trade routes and ports are strategic assets.
* Religious legitimacy can be used to reinforce rule.
* Old houses and noble bloodlines still matter, even when the real power has shifted.

## Continuity notes
* The Great War is the clean dividing line for older house structures.
* The Convergence is the second major break in the political timeline.
* [[The Duchy of Bunelia|Bunelia]] is the dominant power following The Isles of Fate.
