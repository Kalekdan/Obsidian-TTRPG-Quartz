Erlanor Valhorn (known as Erl to his colleagues) is a half-elf wizard (played by [[Joe]]) who has spent his entire adult life studying the arcane with the single goal to know more than any wizard before him. Erlanor has studied since his youth and now naively believes himself to have learned as much knowledge from books as possible. He therefore is venturing into the world to learn from real encounters. Everything he sees, is through the lens of an academic study.

This unfortunately means he has very little experience of any real action, so relies on his intelligence and charm to avoid being the direct target of confrontation.

His familiar, [[Kio]], is an owl, who Erlanor created magically as a young wizard, much to the amazement of his peers. He has not returned [[Kio]] to the pocket dimension since creating her, and treats her much like a living pet. Were she to die and be recreated, she would be a 'new' owl, and since Erlanor grew up with her, he avoids putting her in danger at all costs.

When Erlanor loses Kio in [[The Curse of Strahd - Session 6]], he is distraught.

##  Background 
![[erlanor_icon.png|200]]
Erlanor studied at the [[The College of Arcane History|College of Arcane History]] in [[Pinapota]] from a young age after running away from his home, and an abusive family, in the farms just outside the city. He pretended to be the orphan of a wealthy family, and when his aptitude for learning was noticed, most colleagues chose to let his true history be overlooked. A few decades later, Erlanor left the college, with the intention of expanding his knowledge further by heading into the real world and real adventures.

Fully aware of the impact status and class has on someone's perception of you, Erlanor often finds himself flaunting his newfound money, and will often default to bribery as a bargaining tool. Everything good that has ever happened to him is built upon the lie of his origin, so he is very aware of what people think about him, and any false accusations do not go down well. He has had to constantly maintain a façade, which takes a mental toll leaving him feeling like he is living a double life, always afraid of being discovered.

Due to the abuse he received at a young age, he is particularly protective of the vulnerable, such as children.
