### **Night at the Stonehill Inn**
That night, while the others sleep, **Zephira** remains uneasy about the stone Grommar is transporting. Driven by suspicion, she decides to **sneak into Grommar’s room** at the inn and search for clues.

Picking the lock carefully, she searches through his belongings. In his clothes, she finds a familiar **shipping note confirming the stone delivery**. In the bedside drawer, she uncovers two more items:
- A formal **introduction letter** from his guild.
- A series of **rough sketches and blueprints**. When pieced together, they appear to form a **large humanoid statue**, though much of the detail is missing. Grommar has attempted to fill in some gaps, adding **dwarven and human features**. Zephira assumes the stones stored in Barthen’s Provisions are **parts of this sculpture**.

---
### **Morning at the Inn**
The next morning, the party regathers in the **Stonehill Inn**, run by a human innkeeper named **Toblin**. Grommar reports the break-in to his room during the night.
Toblin suspects the culprits may have been the **Redbrands**, a dangerous gang based at **The Sleeping Giant tavern** down the road. He warns the party to stay away from them. Recently, they killed a shopkeeper named **Fel Bendrar**, took his wife, and caused his children to disappear.

---
### **An Omen by the Shrine**
Outside the inn, **Pfuk**, the dog Bimblee had befriended earlier, is waiting with a stick in his mouth. Bimblee plays fetch with him, but the stick **curiously veers off course**, landing near a **shrine to Tymora**, goddess of luck and good fortune.
**Solanar casts Detect Magic**—but while the shrine itself isn’t magical, **Pfuk glows faintly with magic**, raising further questions.

---

### **Return to the Crossroads**
The group decides to follow up on **Elmina’s concern for Gundren**, returning to the site of the earlier goblin ambush. They take the wagon and **Elmina’s oxen** with them.
At the crossroads, they find a grim sight: a **dead goblin hanging in a net**, with a **crow pecking at its remains.** Solanar notices the crow has a **strange red feather** that seems to **pulse** every time it takes a bite. The goblin also has **Solanar’s dagger**, the same one lost in the earlier battle, **lodged in its eye**.
Bimblee casts **Speak with Animals** and talks to the crow. It responds cryptically, repeating the word **“blood”,** then adds, _“We are coming for your blood.”_ The bird flies off and vanishes in a **bloody mist**.

---
### **Approaching Cragmaw Hideout**
The party tracks goblin footprints to the nearby **Cragmaw Hideout**. Solanar listens carefully and hears **at least two goblins whispering nearby**, planning an ambush.

Before combat begins, **Zephira tries to give Bimblee a pep talk**, after her struggles in the previous fight—but her words come off as a bit harsh: _“Stick with me in the shadows, and I’ll help you.”_
They sneak up on the goblins. One has a **set of keys** on his belt and a **bone charm** at his waist.

A **fight breaks out**. Grommar is seriously injured and goes down, but the party manages to **stabilize him**. Solanar and Bimblee search the surrounding area for herbs and manage to gather **three small healing bundles.** Sol uses one to bring Grommar back to consciousness.
They loot the keys from the fallen goblins and press on.

---

### **Into the Cave**
Entering the hideout, the air is **damp and heavy with the smell of animals**. Just inside, they find **three chained wolves**, snarling and tugging at their restraints. The wolves are held by an iron rod embedded in the stone. Bimblee casts a **magical wolf howl**, which unnerves and briefly calms the beasts.

Further in, they spot a **goblin standing on a rope bridge** spanning the cave. Bimblee uses magic to **create an illusionary smell** on the bridge, which distracts the goblin and lures him away.

---

### **A Deal with Yeemick**
As they continue through the tunnels, the party stumbles upon a larger chamber filled with **goblins and wolves**. They backtrack and climb to a higher vantage via the bridge, trying a different path.

Unfortunately, **Solanar and Zephira fail their stealth**, alerting the goblins. Thinking quickly, they **begin to flirt with the goblin leader**, **Yeemick**, attempting to fluster and distract him. They learn that:
- A **larger goblin named Clarg** is the real boss, located deeper inside.
- **Yeemick** is ambitious and wants Clarg removed so he can take over.
- In return, Yeemick (and Urk the guard) promises to **release a human prisoner**—though he says **they no longer have the dwarf** (presumably Gundren).

Sol and Zephira suggest **working together to kill Clarg**, offering to let Yeemick land the final blow. He agrees. According to Yeemick, the best route to Clarg is **through the wolf pens**, as it provides better cover.

---
### **A Plan to Betray**
Despite their agreement, the party quietly decides not to trust Yeemick. Instead, they plan to **ambush him and Urk**, one of his allies. Their plan: **let them climb down a rope**—then **cut it while they’re still on it**, dropping them and seizing control of the situation.
