##### Backstory
Sol was born during a rare dawn eclipse, an event interpreted as a powerful omen. The priest of Lathander believed the child would walk a path between light and shadow. Sol grew up with a quiet reverence for the rising sun and a strange spiritual attunement to its presence. Misunderstood and isolated by his community, he chose exile at sixteen, retreating to the eastern hills to live a solitary life in communion with the dawn.

For years, he prayed for the world, not himself. Then came the dream—the sun lost, a creeping darkness consuming the land, and a voice calling out from beneath the earth. Interpreting this as a divine summons, Sol left his shrine and began a new kind of pilgrimage: one not of isolation, but of service. He walks now toward Phandalin, believing that his vision is only the beginning of something far greater.
##### Motivations
Wishes to understand the dream, and spread light through the darkest recesses of the world. He wishes to save all those he can from darkness, and will liberate over destroy wherever possible. He feels he must live up to the expectations of his god, as this is the only life he has ever really known.
##### Fears
He fears darkness winning over light - this can often present as a fear of the dark, though he is not afraid of the dark as long as he has a way to stop it.
##### Ideals, Flaws & Bonds
###### Ideals
Sol will do whatever he can to save and liberate people, even when they are beyond saving.
###### Flaws
Sol guards those he cares about with fierce devotion, often stepping in before others have the chance to act or grow. This overprotectiveness can manifest as controlling behavior—especially when matters of faith, sacred places, or relics are involved. He believes the divine entrusted these things to him, and struggles to let others share in their care or interpretation. To his allies, this can make him seem possessive, even selfish—though in his heart, he believes he’s simply keeping the light safe.
###### Bonds
He left a small stone shrine in the hills—his spiritual home. He dreams of returning only once his mission is complete.
