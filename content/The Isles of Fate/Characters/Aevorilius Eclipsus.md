Aevorilius is a human fighter played by [[Lucas]].

As the son of the Duke, he was raised in Bunelia, and grew up their until he attempted to stage a coup to overthrow the Duke for the sake of maintaining a semblance of the power that his father was whittling away. When uncovered, this caused a rift in the already strained relationship between the father and son, and Aevorilius (Aevor) was disowned.
###  Questions to clarify 
1.  What is Aevor doing in Pinapota? Is he looking for someone/something or just trying to get away from his family? 

> Aevor fled Bunelia after a couple of weeks from the shame that his birth rite was taken from him- he debated staying in Bunelia and amassing a peasant force to overthrow his father, but after spending some time in various village taverns it was clear his upper class charisma and indignant self righteousness wasn’t going to win over any local folks over to his cause (especially when most of the folk are either apathetic or quite like his Duke father). Additionally, the idea of leading some populist uprising would potentially destabilise the duchy and its finances further- Aevor’s objectives are twofold; securing his inheritance as rightful heir, but equally making sure that his inheritance isn’t weakened further and rife for external encroachment by neighbouring powers.
>
> He instead decided that the best course of action would be to take the Bunelia fief through delicate political means; as he had been excommunicated from Bunelia politics, the only option was Pinapota. As the capital city, he believes he might be able to find favour with more powerful lords or princes in Pinapota who could help engineer his position back to Bunelian heir. He understands, of course, that having a foreign power install him as the usurper duke of Bunelia would inevitably have implications for the Duchy and himself, so needs to be careful to not be installed in Bunelia merely as a puppet - that would have worse implications than leading a populist uprising within Bunelia.
>
> He dislikes the idea of resorting to such underhanded options in order to secure his birth rite, but understands it as necessary and for the greater good lest he let his father lead it to a slow financial ruin. He is, consequently, fanatical about his aims of usurpation, and will likely kill those who pose obstacles to him if it is deemed necessary to.

2.  Did Aevor grow up with anyone, or have any notable people from his past?

> The one small piece of leverage he has within his father’s court is [[Helvetria]], the chief botanist. As Aevor’s mother died in childbirth, and Aevorilius had no other children to make friends with when growing up, the 45 year old groundskeeper/botanist Helvetria who would look after the castle’s wildlife, took up a motherly mantle to Aevorilius, often finding him in the well-maintained gardens when he was upset and teaching him about the wildlife and flowers. Aevor’s appreciation of nature (and the gift of his crow, Void) come from Helvetria. As the most skilled botanist in the realm, Aevor (without his father’s knowing) was provided a wealth of knowledge on everything chemical and natural, from natural poisons to low level animal speak. She loves Aevor like a mother, and is apathetic to his father the Duke; she’s appreciative that he funds well her botany and maintains the gardens, but resentful that he was a poor father to Aevor. If there were to be a usurpation, Aevor could likely count on her to provide internal support.
