###  Phase 1: Initial Confrontation 
[[Surzak's Monologue]]: The lich notices the players and briefly monologues about his tragic past and his desperate need to bring his family back, framing the encounter as a moral dilemma. He believes what he is doing is right, and that the players are the villains for trying to stop him.

Ranged wont hurt him within the machine (until 30dmg done to sheild)

Combat Begins: The lich engages the players, using powerful spells and minions to stall them. His goal is to buy time until the machine completes its ritual. He may have undead minions (zombies, skeletons, or even a bone naga) guarding the machine.

Arcane Machine's Influence: As the battle progresses, the machine occasionally pulses, sending out waves of planar energy. Roll a d6 at the end of each round:

* 1-2: A rift opens, summoning a weak creature from another plane (e.g., a bone golem, 2x ghoul).
* 3-4: The area is affected by a random planar shift (e.g., gravity reverses for a turn, everyone teleports 30 feet randomly, pillar explodes (2d6 10ft radius).
* 5-6: The planar barrier weakens further, causing reality to flicker; players may see visions of other planes or hear distant screams - machine progresses

###  Phase 2: Machine Manipulation 
Player Choices: Players can interact with the machine in various ways, depending on their skills and abilities:

Arcana Check (DC 18): A successful check allows the player to understand how the machine works and identify weak points.
Disable Device: Rogues or characters with Thieves’ Tools can attempt to disable the machine. Each successful check reduces the time before the machine completes its ritual.
Distraction/Deception: Players might try to distract the lich or deceive him into making a mistake. A successful Charisma (Deception) or Charisma (Persuasion) check might cause the lich to lose concentration, giving players an opening.
Lich’s Desperation: If the players make significant progress in disabling the machine or hurting the lich, he becomes more desperate, unleashing more powerful spells or calling upon additional minions (e.g., summoning shadows or a wraith).

###  Phase 3: Critical Decision Point 
Machine Reaches 75% Completion: As the machine nears completion, the energy it emits becomes more unstable, and the players have a critical decision to make:

Destroy the Machine: If the players decide to destroy the machine by targeting the gem, it triggers a catastrophic explosion. The planes start to collapse, and the true big bad, the evil god, begins to break through. The players now face a race against time to escape or stop the god’s arrival.

Let the Machine Complete: If the players allow the machine to finish, the lich's family returns, but something goes wrong. The revived wife and child are twisted and corrupted by their time in the afterlife, turning them into powerful undead (e.g., revenant, banshee). The lich, horrified by what he’s done, becomes momentarily vulnerable as he grapples with the reality of his actions.
Final Battle: Regardless of the choice, a final battle ensues. If the machine is destroyed, the players face off against the weakened lich and the rapidly emerging god (perhaps an aspect or avatar with limited power). If the machine completes, they must deal with the lich and his undead family.

#### Aftermath and Consequences:
* **Destroying the Machine:** The players may have saved the multiverse from planar collapse, but they now face the wrath of an emerging god. This can lead to a campaign epilogue where the players must find a way to stop the god from fully manifesting.

* **Letting the Machine Complete:** The players may have stopped the lich’s plan, but at a terrible cost. The lich may be defeated, but his undead family poses a new threat, and the moral consequences of their decision will haunt the party.

* **Alternate Endings:** If the players find a way to disable the machine without destroying it, perhaps by using a high-level spell or finding a specific weak point, they might avert both disasters, leading to a more bittersweet or even hopeful ending where the lich’s family returns safely, or they pass on peacefully, allowing the lich to find closure.
