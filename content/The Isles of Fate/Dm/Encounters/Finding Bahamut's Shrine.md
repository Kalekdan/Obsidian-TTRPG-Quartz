The shrine is located within the courthouse, in a secret room that can be accessed by hitting a brick with a gavel. In the shrine, Father T will recieve a coin, with a dragon on one face, and a skull on the other. It will answer questions from Bahamut when tossed (6 charges a day).

> The Platinum Statue: In the center of the shrine stands a magnificent, life-sized statue of Bahamut, sculpted from glistening platinum. The statue radiates a gentle, silvery light that fills the chamber. Bahamut's eyes appear to follow those who enter, giving a sense of his watchful presence.
> 
> Ornate Mosaics: The walls are adorned with intricately detailed mosaics depicting scenes of justice, protection, and righteous battles. These vibrant, multi-colored mosaics bring to life the virtues and principles associated with Bahamut.
> 
> Central Brazier: At the foot of the statue, there's a central brazier. When ignited with the divine fire from Bahamut's eternal flame, this brazier emits a brilliant, blue-white flame. It symbolizes the eternal light of Bahamut, and it is used in the rekindling ritual.
> 
> Starry Ceiling: The ceiling features a remarkable celestial design, with countless stars and constellations meticulously painted. The star patterns on the ceiling contain hidden clues and references to the celestial event needed to reveal the shrine's entrance.
> 
> Altar of Offerings: To the side of the chamber, there is an altar where devotees can place offerings to Bahamut. > The altar is adorned with small statues, vases, and tokens left by those seeking the Platinum Dragon's blessings.
> 
> 
> Anointed Well: In a corner of the shrine, there is a small, luminous well. The waters of this well are believed to be blessed by Bahamut, and it is customary for visitors to take a sip as a sign of devotion and protection.
> 
> The entire shrine exudes a sense of sanctity, tranquility, and divine presence. It is a place of deep reflection, prayer, and devotion for those who rekindle the sacred flame and receive Bahamut's blessings. The hidden nature of the shrine beneath the courthouse adds to its mystique and reinforces its role as a place of quiet reverence and divine connection.
