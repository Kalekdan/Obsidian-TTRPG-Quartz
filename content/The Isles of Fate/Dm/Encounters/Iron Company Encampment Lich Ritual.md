###  The Iron Company Encampment 
> As you approach the clearing in the dense forest, you spot a small encampment beside a swiftly flowing river. A cliff on the far side of the river overlooks the encampment. The encampment is surrounded by trees on all sides, their leaves casting dappled shadows across the clearing. A few small paths lead from the edge of the clearing to the huts, which are made from rough-hewn timber and thatched with straw. ++ Perception DC 10. |It seems underpopulated, perhaps 2 or 3 people.++
>
>There are 5 or 6 huts in total, arranged in a rough circle in the centre of the clearing. Smoke rises from a small fire pit in the centre, and you can hear the faint murmur of voices as the mercenaries inside chat amongst themselves. The huts themselves appear to be fairly new, and relatively temporary, but still sturdy enough to provide shelter from the elements.

[[Iron Company Encampment]]

Within the camp - there is a lockbox containing 30 gold, and a plan of the Temple of Resplendence, and Pinapota Library.

In the centre of the encampment is a large empty cage.

There is also a post in the ground with open shackles attached. 

###  Lich Ritual 
>The temporary mercenary encampment is shrouded in an eerie atmosphere as the ritual to become a lich unfolds. At the center of the encampment, a dark and foreboding altar has been erected, adorned with macabre symbols and lit by flickering, sickly green flames. The air is heavy with the scent of incense, infused with the essence of death and decay.
>
>The BBEG, adorned in tattered robes that seem to absorb the light around them, stands before the altar. They hold a blackened grimoire, its pages filled with arcane symbols and forbidden knowledge. With each carefully enunciated word, the BBEG's voice resonates with a chilling, otherworldly cadence.
>
>Surrounding the BBEG are several mercenaries, their eyes glazed over, their souls ensnared by the BBEG's dark influence. They are bound by sinister, runic chains, forming a circle around the altar, their life force gradually draining into the dark magic at work.
>
>As the ritual progresses, the BBEG begins to extract the life essence of the mercenaries, channeling it into the phylactery—a sinister vessel capable of housing their fragmented soul. The phylactery, a grotesque object forged from obsidian and adorned with necrotic runes, pulses with a malevolent energy as it greedily absorbs the life force.
>
>The encampment is bathed in an eerie glow, the surrounding trees casting long, distorted shadows that seem to twist and writhe with unseen entities. The very fabric of the encampment becomes permeated with the essence of death, as the boundary between the material plane and the realm of undeath begins to waver.
>
>As the ritual nears its climax, the BBEG's form becomes enveloped in a swirling miasma of dark energy. Their eyes flare with an unholy light, their body contorting and writhing in a grotesque dance of agony and ecstasy. With a final incantation, the BBEG relinquishes their mortal ties, surrendering to the dark forces that beckon them.
>
>At that pivotal moment, the BBEG's physical form collapses, their life force seeping into the phylactery, and their essence merges with the necromantic energies. In that instant, the BBEG's transformation into a lich is complete. The encampment falls silent, the air thick with a newfound malevolence, as the newly transformed lich rises from their former self's ashes.

####  Encounter 
Gate is locked (can be unlocked with large key or DC 10, or 10 dmg (AC 10))

There are a 4 members of the iron company who are frozen watching the ritual. One of them is killed every few minutes, and their soul is pulled into the relic of undeath. 1 side of the relic lights up for each killed. If the party breaks his concentration, it will take an action to regain it, and the iron company mercenaries regain some control.

Each member that is killed, spawns a [Flameskull](https://5e.tools/bestiary.html#flameskull_mm).

Felran, Bramble and Bron hear Visil speak to them, telling them to target the mercenaries. 

If the party are able to interrupt the ritual, no more flame skulls will spawn, Surzak will attempt to flee.

At the end of the ritual, the party see several faces in the smoke. An arcana check (DC 16) can tell this was a glimpse into another plane:
* Young half elf boy (Elword)
* Elven lady (Fayeth)
* Duke Eclipsus
* A wolf snarling
* Alexia Whiteshield
* An owlbear
* Sarlen (Felran's old teacher)
* Bramble
