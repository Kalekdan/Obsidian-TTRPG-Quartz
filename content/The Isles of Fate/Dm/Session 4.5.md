#### Introduction
You start the session by describing the character's current location and situation. For example, "You find yourself walking through a quiet alleyway when suddenly, you feel a sharp pain on the back of your head. The world spins and goes black. You wake up a few moments later, your hands tied behind your back, and you're lying on the ground in a dark and unfamiliar room."

#### Discovering the Kidnappers
The character needs to figure out who kidnapped them and why. The room they're in might have clues, like discarded weapons or clothing belonging to the kidnappers. Or they could overhear a conversation from another room, giving them a clue to their location. As they search for clues, they may also discover that some of their money is missing.

#### Escape Attempt
Once the character has gathered enough information, they may attempt to escape from their captors. This could involve picking the lock on their handcuffs, convincing a guard to let them go, or trying to break down a door or window. You can set up obstacles and challenges for the player to overcome, such as guards or traps, to make the escape attempt more exciting.

#### Confronting the Kidnappers
After the character successfully escapes, they will likely want to confront their kidnappers and try to get their stolen money back. This could lead to a combat encounter, where the character faces off against the kidnappers and their allies. Alternatively, the character may try to negotiate with the kidnappers, or gather more information to use against them later.

#### Conclusion
Once the encounter is resolved, you can wrap up the session by describing what happens next. For example, the character may be able to recover their stolen money, but the kidnappers manage to escape. Or they may be able to take down the entire criminal group, but not before sustaining injuries. Whatever happens, make sure to give the player a satisfying conclusion to their adventure.
