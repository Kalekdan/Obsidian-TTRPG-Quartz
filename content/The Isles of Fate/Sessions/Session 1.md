Bramble and Echo are inspecting a store with a collection of oddities. In particular there is a metal bottle, at 50gp it's too much however, even when reduced to twenty. As the various individuals shop in the market, they start to notice more and more mist. They hear a commotion and see three individuals with blue face tattoos start to grab a priest walking through the market. Theo notices that although the mist is thicker in that direction an old man with a walking stick seems to be the source of the mist. 

As Bramble, Echo and Theo run towards the priest and three abductors, Theo calls out for them to stop and one of them strides towards Theo with his sword drawn. Jack calls out to the largest of the 3 guards and manages to charm him, but doesn't manage to get him to release his grip on the priest

 Echo steps in front of Theo and grabs the man pumping electricity into him. Theo steps to the side and swings down with his sword cleaving into him and adding to the lightning overloading the man's system.

Bramble runs up and grapples the closest thug. And Echo runs on and calls lightning down on the thug pulling the priest away.

Jack slips into a nearby building to observe from a distance. As the chaos is unfolding, Felran steps forwards and plants his staff into the cobbled ground unleashing a burst of energy that narrowly misses. He continues to advance unleashing energy towards the abductors.

As the grappled thug struggles to free himself from Brambles grip. Theo brings his sword round again, and cleaves the man from shoulder to navel. He leaves the crackling sword in the body and sprints over to the priest, inspiring him with a quick story.

Bramble pulls Theo's sword free and runs over to the guard that Echo has been fighting. He swings at him, but the same electricity that Theo was drawing from the sword is absent. The largest guard is still wondering around, confused and unsure of who he should be helping.

Theo snarls at Bramble to give back his sword and promptly retrieves it. Jack throws a knife down at the second guard, helping to slay him. But starts to wonder if he's actually helping the right people. And starts to throw spells in Brambles direction, which fortunately miss.

The largest guard finally shakes free of Jack's spell, and sets about tearing the party apart with his greatsword. Theo runs away healing himself as Bramble and Echo weather the punishment and Feltran and Jack (now clearer on the situation) assist from a distance.

Finally as the group is on it's last legs, they manage to kill the largest final guard.

Theo ushers them into a nearby building and off the street. Bramble goes to drag one of the bodies with them, before he's stopped. Bramble and Felran(?) Loot the bodies and find several keys, some smaller, some bigger, and an ornate knife with a wavy blade.

Inside, they question the priest who, who thanks them and reveals himself to be Bishop Fearson. He reveals that he has no idea who the men who attacked him were. However, he does note that Eric Thormund has similar face tattoos and that he can often be found at a pub called the rusty anvil. Bishop Fearson reveals that his church (as a priest of the matron) are guarding several powerful relics and that it was these the thugs were after. 

As the party tried to decide whether they should depart or go to the rusty anvil, Bishop Fearson's eyes roll back in his head. He says "beware a friend with Jade tidings for they are no friend at all". As his eyes roll back forwards he has no recollection of this event. 

He invites the group to the temple of Resplendence if they want to find out more and then departs. After some back and forth the group finally decide to follow, with an eager Echo and Bramble leading the way, and Jack reluctantly following along. Bramble sits on Echo’s shoulders as they conspicuously head through the streets. As they arrive, most of the party go to head in, Jack hangs back and disguises himself as a priest. Felran uses this time to cast an illusory spell and make himself look like a pillar.

In the temple, the party asks Bishop Fearson if they can see the relics that the muggers might be after, but the Bishop is unwilling to show them as they’re too dangerous, nor offer them as a reward for services rendered. Felran hides at the back of this temple through this exchange. Jack then strides in, and proclaims himself an inspector, demanding to see the artifacts and ensure their safe storage. Fearson appears to believe him, but even so is unwilling to take anyone to see the artifacts.
