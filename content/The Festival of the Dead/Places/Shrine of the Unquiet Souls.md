The shrine of the unquiet souls is where a lot of the story is focused. They pick [[The Hero]] each year, and are in charge of the festival. **They also commune with the dead, and can help players commune.** Their symbol is a broken hourglass.
Some of the monks include:
	- [[Brother Tim Hardy]] (acolyte)

	- Admits that sometimes he hears whispers through the floorboards during evening prayers

	- Stern, devoted, and fearful of divine wrath.        
	    - Knows there is _something_ in the crypt — a sacred relic, or perhaps a warded soul — but has not descended in years.
	    - Believes it is forbidden to enter except by tradition, “when the Hero passes.”
	- To Rhys - 'You carry a great burden'
- [[Elaris the Dying Monk]]

### Gossip
- Jaskia was chosen three months ago through the “Dream of Ash and Fire” (a prophetic vision).
- The veil beneath the cliffs is sealed with a warding bell — the “Last Toll.”  
    It has begun to _crack_, though only the monks know this.
- The monks have noticed more “early awakenings” — spirits flickering before the festival.
### Hooks
- Kalem might privately ask the party to investigate the graveyards for disturbances.
- Mirell subtly discourages questions about the demon, calling it “unwise curiosity.” (because he highlights the weakness of man)


### The Altar and Signs of Disturbance
**Religion (DC 13):** The party can tell this Shrine is unusually devoted to _maintaining the veil_ — its entire purpose is to ensure the dead do not linger.
- **Arcana or Perception (DC 14):** The marble around the altar bears faint traces of necromantic energy — not overtly evil, but _distorted_, like a healing ritual gone wrong.
- **Investigation (DC 13):** The floor near the altar bears faint drag marks leading toward a side passage that ends at a sealed stair.
- **Religion (DC 15):** The wards on the stair are old but legitimate — intended to _seal off_ the crypt except during the Festival.
The **Abbess** insists the lower levels are a “sacred ossuary” that none are permitted to disturb before the Festival, for fear of “calling the dead early." - If the players manage to enter, souls of dead monks and heroes arise and attack - they seem to be losing control of any sentience
- *'I was there at Greenhollow... I know what you did'* - solemn not angry, before losing control and insanity kicks in

### **The Hall of Memorials**

### **Tone:** Sacred and sorrowful. A corridor lined with reliefs and old names.

Players can explore or question monks.
**Key Details:**
- Each wall relief shows a past Hero defeating the demon. The last panel is unfinished — intended for Jaskia.
- Religion or History check (DC 13): The festival’s earliest records are _fragmentary_, with signs of an older, darker origin.
- One relief shows a man with **[[Elaris the Dying Monk|Elaris]]’s likeness** among the monks of decades past — a visual clue that he’s far older than he should be.

##### **The Chamber of Rest (Jaskia)**
A vaulted crypt of smooth black stone lies at the base. The Shrine’s symbol — **the broken hourglass** — is carved on every pillar.
At its center lies **Jaskia Boneheart**, bound in silver runes and black salt.  
Her chest rises faintly, but her skin is cold; the veins beneath glow faint blue.
**When the players approach:**
- Jaskia stirs, eyes flicking open.
- She whispers: _“It wasn’t meant to be this way… he said I’d sleep until the Festival…”_
- She recognizes none of them, and fades again, the sigils pulsing faintly.
**Checks / Discoveries:**
- **Arcana (DC 15):** The ritual is necromantic preservation — a halfway state between life and death.
- **Religion (DC 13):** The runes are written in an old Shrine script — the style used by monks two or more generations ago.
- [[Jaskia's Ember]] is on her person - a particularly detailed blade
