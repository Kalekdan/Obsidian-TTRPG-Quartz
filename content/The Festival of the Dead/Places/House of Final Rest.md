The **House of Final Rest** is where the dying are cared for in peace before the Festival of the Dead. Every year, monks ensure their bodies are properly blessed so their spirits can rise peacefully when the veil thins.

The air is quiet, reverent — yet something about it feels off. Candles burn longer than they should. The smell of incense barely masks the faint rot beneath. And one monk, Elaris, has not left his room in weeks.

## **Scene 1 – Arrival**

### **Tone:** Muted, respectful, and heavy with unspoken fear.

**Read or paraphrase:**

> The House of Final Rest perches on the cliff below the Shrine — its walls streaked with sea-salt and age. Wind sighs through the narrow windows, carrying the slow tolling of bells. Inside, robed monks move silently among the dying, whispering prayers of release.

**Atmosphere:**
- The sound of waves echoes like distant breathing.
- Incense mingles with brine and decay.
- Candles flicker strangely, as if drawing light from unseen movement.
---
### **NPCs Present**
- **Caretaker Lorrin:** Older woman, calm and kind.
    - Guides the dying through their final rites.
    - Avoids speaking of Elaris directly, saying only, _“He prays in his own way now.”_
- **Two Silent Attendants:**
    - Masked acolytes who tend to bodies; avoid conversation.
    - If questioned: _“Brother Elaris sees beyond the veil — we must not disturb him.”_

## **Scene 2 – Exploring the Halls**
The players can wander, observe, and interact — this scene’s goal is **unease through empathy**.
**Details & Discoveries:**
- **Religion (DC 12):** The prayers carved into the walls are older than standard liturgy — some verses altered to speak of _“joining”_ or _“merging”_ with the departed.
- **Perception (DC 13):** Candles near the western hallway burn with faint blue flame.
- **Arcana (DC 14):** A subtle necrotic field lingers near one locked door — protective, not harmful.
- **Investigation (DC 14):** You notice patterns of salt across thresholds — a ward to keep spirits _in,_ not _out._
If players ask who lives behind the locked door, they’ll hear:
> “That is Brother Elaris’s room. He tends to the dying who cannot let go.”

## **Scene 3 – Meeting Elaris**
_(If the players insist on meeting him — or are invited inside.)_
**Tone:** Gentle melancholy with a wrongness that’s hard to name.
**Read or paraphrase:**
> The chamber smells of wax, parchment, and something faintly metallic. Hundreds of candles line the walls. A frail monk kneels in the center, writing on the floor with black ink that seems to absorb the light. His eyes are pale silver, his smile tired but kind.

**Elaris’s demeanor:**
- Warm, soft-spoken, slightly detached.
- Seems lucid but occasionally drifts mid-sentence.
- Speaks of death not as an ending, but as _“the stillness before dawn.”_
In Elaris' room players may find:
- History of the festival tome (describes [[Anhural]])
- [[The Frozen Hourglass]]

---

### **Roleplay Beats (use what fits your players’ tone):**
- **General Conversation:**
    > “I once guided souls to their rest. Now I prepare myself for mine.”
- **If asked about the Festival:**
    > “A sacred time. The dead walk, and we are reminded that love need not fade — only change its shape.”
- **If asked about the Shrine:**
    > “The Shrine is a place of order. But death is not order — it is longing. The monks have forgotten that.”
- **If asked about Jaskia or the Hero:**
    > “Ah… the chosen one. May her courage outlive her flesh.” _(He smiles faintly, but his hand trembles.)_
- **If pressed about the strange energy:**
    > “Do not mistake comfort for corruption, friends. When the dying whisper, I only listen.”
- **If attempt combat:**
	- Shades will emerge from the walls to defend him
		- Treat Elaris as a **Priest (MM)** with 2 Warlock spells: _Arms of Hadar_, _Hold Person_.
		- Add 2–3 **Shades** (homebrewed Wraith-like spirits) that defend him reflexively.
	- He does not try to kill the party — he only defends himself until they flee or strike him down.
	- If he dies, his last words are:
		- “Do not mistake comfort for corruption, friends. When the dying whisper, I only listen.”
	- If he lives, he can later appear as a withered half-undead prophet during the Festival itself.

---
### **Checks / Clues**
Subtle, not explicit — they’ll make sense later after the Shrine.
- **Insight (DC 14):** He’s telling the truth as _he believes it_ — but he’s not well. There’s grief behind his calm.
- **Arcana (DC 15):** His room hums faintly with the same necrotic-preservative magic as the Shrine’s altar (foreshadowing his role in Jaskia’s preservation).
- **Religion (DC 13):** The candlelight here flickers unnaturally — divine light twisted by something half-spiritual, half-mortal.

## **Scene 4 – Departing the House**
Elaris won’t stop them from leaving — he’s courteous, even wistful.
**As they go:**
- A cold wind blows down the corridor.
- The bells of the Shrine ring out of time again.
- A dying man whispers in a woman’s voice: _“He waits below the waves…”_
Players might think it’s coincidence. Later, they’ll realize it was **Anhural** reaching through Elaris’s dreams.

---
## **Session End Beat**
- As they step back into the fog, the House’s windows flicker — candlelight pulsing like a heartbeat.
- The sea crashes below, and for a moment, they swear they see figures standing in the surf.
- Back in town, townsfolk avert their eyes when they mention visiting the House.
