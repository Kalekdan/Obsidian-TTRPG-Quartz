### Sharing the note with Abess
- The 'veil' - are an old sect within the church
	- consider themselves servants of [[Anhural]] - the first to rebel death
	- They were cast out of the church a long time ago
Where the Church teaches:
> The dead must pass on, and we honour them by remembering.

The Veil believes:
> The dead _do not wish to pass on_. The boundary is artificial.

They see death as **a separation imposed by divine law**, not a natural truth.
Anhural was **the first mortal to refuse the lie of death**. - To them, Anhural is not a demon. He is a **liberator of the dead**.

## A vision in the night
Nyx wakes up having heard her name called again, from the coast. If they go to the port, a fisherman swears he saw a figure standing on the breakwater.
If they investigate, they will see her husband, alive but drowned — **stuck between life and death**. His voice sounds distant, like it carries through water.

He has spoken with the dead from his ship and learned:
- **They could not pass on.** Something in the world is **holding the dead close to the living**.
- Some of the drowned speak of **figures in black veils performing rites along the coast**, whispering prayers not to the gods of death, but to **Anhural**. - they retreated each night to a cave.
- The spirits say the boundary between life and death is **growing thin**, especially as the **Festival of the Dead approaches**.
- If the ritual the Veil prepares succeeds, **many of the dead will remain**, unable to cross into the afterlife.
- And something else: **he feels the same force that keeps him alive pulling at the world itself**. 
He warns Nyx quietly:
> “What holds me here… they are trying to make it happen to everyone.”
