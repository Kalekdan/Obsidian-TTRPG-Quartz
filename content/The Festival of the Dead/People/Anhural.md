**"The First to Defy the End."**

![[Pasted image 20250711090645.png|300]]

Long before Virellis existed, before kingdoms had names, there was a man — or something close to one — known as **Anhural**.

He was a high priest, scholar, or oracle who _refused to accept death as final_. His **twin** (or lover, or child) was taken by a **death god**, one of the old and cold ones, and Anhural **petitioned endlessly** for their return.

When denied, he did the unthinkable:  
He **entered the realm of the dead** not as a soul, but as a living intruder, and tried to **steal back the dead**.

In doing so, he **tore open the veil** for the first time.

This **ancient act of defiance** is what birthed the **Festival of the Dead** — not as a tradition, but as a _curse_. Once a year, the veil he pierced grows thin again, echoing the day the boundaries of death were broken.

The death god, enraged, did not kill Anhural.  
Instead, it **twisted him** —

- **Disfigured his body** until it resembled all the souls he tried to keep.
- **Cursed him to remember every name** ever lost to the veil.
- **Condemned him to live forever in the shadow of the rift he created.**

Now, Anhural **seeks to complete what he started**:

> “If I cannot bring one back, I will bring them all. If I cannot walk with the living, the living shall walk with the dead.”
