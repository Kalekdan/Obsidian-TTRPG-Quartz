## **The Festival of the Dead & The Endless Night**

### **The Tradition**

Every year, for one sacred day, the veil between life and death thins. The dead awaken, walking the streets alongside the living in a grand **Festival of the Dead.** Families reunite, lovers share final words, and forgotten names are spoken once more. It is a time of **joy, reverence, and remembrance.**

But at the end of the festival, **something terrible always arrives.**

As the last lanterns flicker, **a demon steps forth from the veil.** It speaks in whispers and temptation, urging the dead to remain. It lingers in the alleys, the shadows, the forgotten corners of the city, promising **a second chance** to those unwilling to leave. And if left unchecked, the dead **lose themselves, becoming part of its ever-growing army.**

To stop this, **a hero is chosen each year by prophecy.** Armed with divine power and fated strength, they stand against the demon, slay it once more, and restore the cycle for another year. It has always worked. It has always been this way.

But this year, the prophecy has failed.

## **The Inciting Incident**

### **The Chosen Hero**

- Days before the Festival, the party arrives in the city. The streets are **overflowing with excitement**—not just for the Festival, but for the **newly chosen Hero.**
- The **Hero is being paraded through the city,** wearing ceremonial armor, flanked by guards and priests.
- The people **believe in them**—they are **the one destined to defeat the demon.**

### **The Assassination**

- **Days later, the Hero is found dead.**
- Whether they were **poisoned, stabbed in their sleep, or killed in the streets,** the city is in shock.
- **The Festival is still happening.** There is no stopping it. But without the Hero, **who will face the demon?**

### **The Hero’s Spirit**

- During the Festival, the dead rise as expected. But **among them is the Hero.**
- The prophecy **never accounted for this.** They should be alive. They should be fighting. Instead, **they walk among the dead, lost, confused, or perhaps at peace.**
- Do they remember their purpose? Do they care?

## **The Party’s Role**

1. **They meet the Hero before their death.** They may even be asked to protect them—or interact with those who wish them harm.
2. **They are there when the assassination occurs—or are blamed for it.**
3. **They must uncover the truth behind the murder.** Was it a mortal plot? Or something far worse?
4. **They must decide what to do about the Hero’s spirit.** Can they convince them to take up their destiny?
5. **If no hero stands, who will face the demon?**
