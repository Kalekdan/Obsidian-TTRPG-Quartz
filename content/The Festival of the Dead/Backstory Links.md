### [[Nyxandra Thalasson]] (Charlie)
Themes: _Love, loss, the ocean’s pull, blurred lines between life and death._  
**How to weave her in:**
- **Early Foreshadowing (in Virellis):**
    - The sea below the cliffs whispers faintly to her at night; she hears names the others don’t.
    - The **Shrine’s relic water** (used for purification) reacts to her — briefly turning black or rippling unnaturally when she nears.
- **The Bandit Quest:**
    - Among the bandits’ stolen goods, a charm made of **driftwood shaped like a heart** — identical to her own — suggests someone has been imitating or studying her magic.
- **Elaris Connection:**
    - He recognizes her aura immediately — her gift mirrors the **necrotic-preservative** power he’s channeling. He’ll call her a _“kindred spirit who hears the same tide.” "Ah, you hear the voices too"_ 
- **The Demon Anhural’s Influence:**
    - The demon’s whispers sound like her husband’s voice at first, tempting her with promises of reunion “if she helps open the veil.”
- **Future Payoff:**
    - Her husband’s soul could become one of Anhural’s earliest thralls — a personal stake in stopping the demon and saving what remains of him.
### [[Rhys Greywood]] (James)
Themes: _Guilt, justice, penance, spiritual reckoning._  
**How to weave him in:**
- **Early Hook (Arrival):**
    - He attends the Festival seeking forgiveness — the spirits of the _Silent Harvest_ villagers could appear when the veil thins.
    - The monks treat him reverently, as penitents are common here — but Abbess Mirell seems _nervous_ around him, sensing his burden.
- **Bandit Quest:**
    - A survivor or descendant from **Greenhollow** (his massacre) is among the bandits — they carry a _Family Knot talisman_ identical to his.
- **Connection to Elaris:**
    - Elaris recognizes his guilt instantly and tempts him with absolution:
        > “The dead do not hate you, Rhys. They only want to be remembered.”
- **Shrine/House Tie:**
    - In the crypt, when near Jaskia’s body, he hears the whispers of his victims — suggesting that if the veil opens fully, they could return.
- **Future Payoff:**
    - He’ll face a choice during the Festival:  
        _help close the veil and let them rest, or open it to seek forgiveness in person — at a terrible cost_
### [[Cricket Abernathy]] (Alicia)
Themes: _Order vs. chaos, life/death balance, identity and self-deception._  
**How to weave her in:**
- **Early Foreshadowing (Arrival):**
    - The **flora around Virellis** is behaving strangely — withering or flowering out of season. Her divine connection to Obad-Hai tingles constantly; nature is “off balance.”
- **Shrine Connection:**
    - The monks mention the **Order of Obad-Hai** once helped bless the Shrine centuries ago — the same symbols appear warped in the crypt’s runes.
- **House of Final Rest:**
    - She senses natural decay being _interfered with._ The dying don’t pass on — their spirits “cling.”
    - Elaris remarks admiringly that she understands “balance,” using Obad-Hai’s teachings to justify his defiance of death.
- **Personal Arc:**
    - Discovering that she’s actually a **gnome** (not halfling) could mirror the campaign’s central motif — _false boundaries between worlds and truths blurred by tradition._
- **Future Payoff:**
    - She might need to perform a ritual that restores natural death — forcing her to confront that _balance sometimes requires letting go._
