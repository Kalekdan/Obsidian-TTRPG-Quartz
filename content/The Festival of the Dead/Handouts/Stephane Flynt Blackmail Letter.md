The parchment is creased, folded many times, the ink uneven—as if written in haste or by a trembling hand. The handwriting is jagged, deliberately obscured to mask its origin.

> _To Stephane Flynt,_
> 
> Your mother rests in the House of Final Rest, under the care of monks who are not always as watchful as they claim. A lantern overturned, a draught of spoiled herbs, a single loosened stair—so many accidents might befall her fragile body.
> 
> I do not wish her harm. I only wish your cooperation. Place your master’s fine work—the blades she shapes with such pride—into the hands of those who wait beyond the road. Leave them where the forest meets the graveyard wall. Do this, and your mother’s peace will remain undisturbed. Fail me, and she will not live to see the Festival’s end.
> 
> There will be no second warning.
> 
> _—A Friend of the Veil_
