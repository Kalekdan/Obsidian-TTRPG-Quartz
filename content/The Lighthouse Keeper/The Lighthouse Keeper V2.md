New version of [[The Lighthouse Keeper]], reworked given I now have more experience DMing and running campaigns, and wanted to make it more reusable, so it's easy to pick up for new groups.

### Prep

- Character sheets (examples can be found in [[Premade Characters]])
- Items (can be physical handouts if wanted)
	- [[Repulsive Necklace]] - magic item
	- [[Necklace note]] - handout
	- [[Bandits note]] - handout
	- [[Love Letter]] - handout
- Maps (* are 'required', ^ could contain combat, but all are effectively optional)
	- The Tasty Battleaxe (Tavern)

## Intro

> The party find themselves entering 'The Tasty Battleaxe', a friendly tavern frequented by adventurers. It is quieter than they might expect given the time of day, but they sit down and the bartender approaches them to see what he can get them.


### The Tasty Battleaxe
When the party place an order, the bartender will let them know that there is no ale (replace 'ale' with whatever they order). They usually get a shipment every few days, but the last few haven't made it. Ships have been crashing against the rocks during deliveries. He suspects foul play. He points out **Preston Carter**, who is looking sad in the corner - his brother (**Donovan**) was on one of the boats which crashed and they haven't seen him in a couple of days.

### The Wreck on the Beach
The party go to the wreck and spot 2 bandits looting. Combat ensues. Investigating the wreck they notice the ship was heading direct to the lighthouse, no sign of trying to correct the course. A high perception may notice one of the bodies still breathing, **Donovan Carter** (Preston's brother). They can try and help him, and he will give them some info about why they crashed, but will remain vague for fear of putting 'his love' (the harpy) at risk. High enough persuasion or intimidation can get more detail. They can continue to the lighthouse via a cave system from the beach through the cliffs.

### The Bridge
The party can go direct to the lighthouse by going over the bridge. When they get there, the bridge has collapsed. They can attempt to find a way to cross the bridge (if they succeed add 2 bandits defending the matriarch). In most cases they fail and fall down into the lake in the cave below.

Alternatively, they may see a note on the bridge posts;
> Bridge under repair, please use alternative route via beach.

They can follow the path to the right down to the beach.

### The Cave
If **arriving from the wreck** side, they encounter the bandits camp, and can loot it finding, some health potions, a +1 weapon, and some of the missing ales from the inn. They can also find a [[Bandits note|note]] with instructions to disarm a trap. A high enough perception will notice a [[Repulsive Necklace|necklace]] (advantage against charmed - can adjust to +1 if there are lots of party members with 'Fey Ancestry') under the pile of notes on the desk. There is also a [[Necklace note|note]] loosely explaining the effect it has.

As they head through, there is a pressure plate which triggers arrows to fire from the wall. Without the note, DC 16 Dex check (bad failure triggers the trap). With the note, a DC 5 Dex to disable the trap.

Alternatively, they can go through the nothern path, and attempt to move the blockade out of the way (acrobatics to climb over DC 15, or athletics DC 15 to move it).

They then get to the lake, which they must pass.

If **arriving from the broken bridge**, they fall straight into the lake.

In the lake is a giant octopus, which they must fight. Beyond the lake they can leave the cave.

### The Lighthouse
On arriving at the lighthouse, they see the light shining. They head inside and meet a dwarf (**Numnet Deepbeard**). He is the lighthouse keeper, but seems not to be fussed by the party, or the fact that ships are crashing. He is very friendly initially, seemingly oblivious to anything wrong. If they ask too many questions, or get too accusational, he will get very defensive and try to attack. If killed, the party can loot his body to find a [[Love Letter]] to the Harpy Matriarch, promising to do whatever he can for her.
